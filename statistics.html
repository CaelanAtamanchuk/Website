My First Time Teaching

In the winter semester of 2025, I taught an informal course on scaling limits of random trees for some colleagues in the math department that was attended by my supervisor Luc Devroye. During the course he was my most diligent student, rapidly taking notes when he heard something that he thought was worth noting. I thought these notes were about the material I was teaching, or perhaps notes on how I could improve as a lecturer. However, he was actually just noting the number of times that I said a curse word during the course because he thought it was funny. The course was twelve lectures long, and here was his report that he kept on a sheet of paper:
I tell people that his final review of the course was this: "Caelan, you rival the great Bruce Reed in terms of ability."

His actual final review was this: "Caelan, you rival the great Bruce Reed in terms of ability to swear during a lecture."
